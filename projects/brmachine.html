BRMachine Operational Tracker

Structured inventory and failure-tracking framework for distributed field operations.

GitHub repository not available for this project.

During my experience working with entertainment and vending machines at BRMachine, I developed a structured operational framework to manage spare parts inventory and track recurring equipment failures across distributed machines installed at multiple commercial locations throughout the region.

The Problem

The business operated multiple machines installed in different commercial locations, which required continuous maintenance and replacement of mechanical and electronic components. Without a structured tracking system, it was difficult to monitor recurring failures, control spare parts usage, or manage technician interventions efficiently.

The absence of operational data made it impossible to identify patterns in equipment failure, evaluate whether repair was more cost-effective than replacement, or hold technician activity accountable in any structured way.

What I Built

I designed a simple but structured tracking framework that gave the team visibility over spare parts consumption, equipment failure patterns, and technician activity. The system allowed weekly tracking of parts used across different machines and locations, and provided a basis for operational decisions regarding repair versus component replacement.

  • Weekly tracking of spare parts usage across machines and locations
  • Identification of recurring failure patterns in mechanical and electronic components
  • Categorisation of failure types to support maintenance prioritisation
  • Allocation control of parts per technician with intervention record tracking
  • Decision logic framework to evaluate repair versus replacement for cost optimisation
